EXPLORE THE GALAXY

Milky Way

Is the galaxy that includes the Solar System, with the name describing the galaxy's appearance from Earth: a hazy band of light seen in the night sky formed from stars that cannot be individually distinguished by the naked eye.

Andromeda

The Andromeda Galaxy, also known as M31, is the closest spiral galaxy to the Milky Way. It spans about 220,000 light-years and is on a collision course with our galaxy, expected to merge in several billion years.

Know more

Sombrero

The Sombrero Galaxy, or M104, is a bright spiral galaxy characterized by its large central bulge and prominent dust lane, giving it the appearance of a sombrero. It’s about 29 million light-years from Earth in the constellation Virgo.

Know more

Whirlpool

The Whirlpool Galaxy, also known as M51, is a stunning spiral galaxy located about 31 million light-years away. Its distinctive spiral arms and interaction with a companion galaxy make it a favorite target for astronomers.

Know more

Triangulum

The Triangulum Galaxy, or M33, is a spiral galaxy located about 3 million light-years away in the constellation Triangulum. It’s the third-largest member of the Local Group of galaxies, after the Milky Way and Andromeda.

Know more

Cartwheel

The Cartwheel Galaxy is a ring galaxy located about 500 million light-years away. It was formed by a collision with a smaller galaxy, which created shock waves that triggered star formation in a ring shape.

Know more

Pinwheel

The Pinwheel Galaxy, or M101, is a grand-design spiral galaxy located 21 million light-years away in the constellation Ursa Major. It’s known for its large, well-defined spiral arms and is a favorite among astrophotographers.

Know more

Messier 87

Messier 87, or M87, is a giant elliptical galaxy in the Virgo Cluster, located 53 million light-years away. It’s famous for its supermassive black hole, which was the first to be imaged by the Event Horizon Telescope.

Know more

Centaurus A

Centaurus A is a peculiar galaxy located about 13 million light-years away. It’s one of the closest active galaxies to Earth and has a supermassive black hole at its center, producing strong radio emissions.

Know more

NGC 1300

NGC 1300 is a barred spiral galaxy located 61 million light-years away in the constellation Eridanus. It’s known for its symmetrical spiral arms and central bar structure, making it a classic example of a barred spiral galaxy.
